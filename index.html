    Hi, I am an Associate Professor at Nagoya Institute of Technology. My research interest lies at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and Cognitive Computational Neuroscience. During my Ph.D., I worked on reliable techniques for predicting diverse individual differences from whole-brain functional connectivity using functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) at the ATR Brain Information Communication Research Laboratory. After obtaining my degree, I have been working on understanding dynamic computations during complex decision-making tasks using machine learning techniques at the Oxford Centre for Human Brain Activity at the University of Oxford, and Department of Psychology at the University of Tokyo. More recently, I investigated the connection between generative AI and the human brain at Osaka University and the Research and Development Center for Large Language Models at National Institute of Informatics
