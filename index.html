    Hi, I am a visiting fellow in the Oxford Centre for Human Brain Activity at the University of Oxford, working with my great supervisors Dr. Laurence Hunt and Dr. Mark Woolrich. My research interest is the intersection of Computational Neuroscience and Artificial Intelligence.

    My long-term research goals are understanding neural substrates of individual differences, especially in the domain of Decision Making. During my Ph.D., I worked on reliable techniques for predicting diverse individual differences from whole-brain functional connectivity using functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI). More recently, I am working on understand a dynamic interaction between different brain regions during complex decision making tasks with state-of-the-art machine learning technique. I have also been working as a and Non-resident Researchers at ATR Brain Information Communication Research Laboratory, and was appointed as a Specially Appointed Researcher of the University of Tokyo.
